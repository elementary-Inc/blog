We've released elementary OS 5.1.6, our monthly roundup of updates and improvements to Hera.

## Bountysource → GitHub Sponsors

If you have used [Bountysource](https://bountysource.com) in the past, you may have received an email announcing new terms of service that included a clause that would forfeit unclaimed bounties. Those controversial terms have since been withdrawn, but it has led us to re-think our relationship with and recommendation of Bountysource.

If you didn't know, Bountysource was acquired by a cryptocurrency company<!-- when?-->. Since then, the quality of service and support has gone downhill, with long lead times, especially when dealing with payouts or issues. This—along with their recent terms of service issue—is unacceptable. As such, we've removed Bountysource from our website, removed any Bountysource integrations with elementary repos on GitHub, withdrawn our funds, and closed our account.

For backers and bounty hunters on Bountysource, **we recommend you also withdraw your funds and close your account** as soon as possible. The future of Bountysource is unclear, and the new ownership has demonstrated they are willing to take advantage of your funds if given the chance.

As an alternative, we recommend checking out [GitHub Sponsors]. You can directly sponsor [organizations like elementary][GitHub Sponsors], as well as individual contributors. For elementary specifically, we have a new $50/month tier that offers the closest equivalent to a bug bounty program: each month, backers at this tier can comment on any elementary issue to get at least an hour of our investigation time. This is a great way to help steer development in the direction you see fit while also directly supporting us.
