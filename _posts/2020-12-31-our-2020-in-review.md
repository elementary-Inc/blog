## January

Hera updates: first batch of updates to elementary OS 5.1, with developer-focused updates for Code, Terminal, and Files.

## Feb

New release process, starting spinning more regular ISOs including HWE. Also minor updates to AppCenter, Files, and Code.

Announced AppCenter for Everyone, smashed the goal on the third day of the campaign. Continued to provide updates through the month.

## March: COVID-19

Ah, March. This month started out looking incredibly promising, with AppCenter for Everyone hitting [190% funded by the end of the campaign](/appcenter-for-everyone-campaign-results)—and then COVID-19 hit the US. As a result, we had to make the difficult decision to postpone the in-person AppCenter for Everyone sprint until it's safe to meet in person. We held a [short online sprint](/appcenter-for-everyone-remote-sprint) that helped us prototype some design, web, and desktop work, but ultimately our scope of work was too ambitious for this format, and required too much real-time collaboration to be practical remotely. We still plan to hold an in-person sprint in the future to achieve this work.

We also pushed out relatively minor updates to Files, System Settings, the Panel and system indicators, and several other apps.

We also debuted our new [Releases](https://releases.elementary.io) tool

5.1.3

## April: Minor Updates

Screen Time & Limits, new Applications Menu category design and multi-touch support. Redesigned System Settings Search, exposing deep searching for individual settings. AppCenter with crash fixes and improvements to performance, extensions support, proxy support, and keyboard navigation.

Minor updates to Videos, Gala, Greeter, Panel, Photos, and Granite.

5.1.4

## May: Quality of Life updates

Quality-of-life improvements to AppCenter and Files. For AppCenter, users can now install updates without administrator permission. System Settings pane improvements, performance and recurring event fixes for Calendar and the Date & Time indicator

5.1.5

## June: Shipping elementary OS on Hardware

June was a particularly busy month! We [launched our OEM program](/now-shipping-elementary-os) along with two retailers of elementary devices: Laptop with Linux and Star Labs. This is a huge milestone and marks a new chapter in the story of elementary OS going forward, and has continued to evolve throughout the year.

I was also [interviewed by Linux++](/linux-plus-plus-interview-cassidy-james-blaede) about elementary, which ended up being a great read. We finally [said goodbye to Bountysource](/goodbye-bountysource-hello-github-sponsors) in June, doubling down on GitHub Sponsors instead.

Smaller updates and fixes to AppCenter, Code, Files, Videos, Calendar, and more. We also started preparing for the transition to elementary OS 6 with developer-facing updates and some apps being targeted to 6.0 only.

5.1.6

## July: Plausible

After a month of trialing it, we completed our [migration to Plausible](/leaving-google-analytics-is-finally-plausible), improving the privacy of visitors to elementary websites.

[Minor updates](/updates-for-july-2020#updates-to-elementary-os-51-hera) to AppCenter, Music indicator, Keyboard settings. 5.1.7

## August: Early Access

In August, we [launched our Early Access program](/updates-for-july-2020#early-access-builds-of-elementary-os-6) for GitHub Sponsors, and teased some of what's to come in elementary OS 6.

Following our launch of Early Access, we announced [experimental builds for Pinebook Pro](/elementary-os-on-pinebook-pro).

## September: Slimbook

The big news in September was [Slimbook joining our retailer program](/devices-slimbook), as well as new models from Laptop with Linux and Star Labs. Behind the scenes, we continued our work on elementary OS 6.

## October

[platform changes coming in elementary OS 6](/platform-changes-in-elementary-os-6), including the new system stylesheet, notifications, and multi-touch gestures. We also shared how and why to [use elementary OS to revitalize a Mac or Windows PC](revitalize-mac-windows-computer/).
